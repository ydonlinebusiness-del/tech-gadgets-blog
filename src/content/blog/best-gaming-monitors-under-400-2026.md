## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best gaming monitor under $400 in 2026?**
The AOC Q27G3XMN is the best gaming monitor under $400 in 2026. It delivers Mini LED HDR 1000 with 336 local dimming zones, 1440p at 180Hz, and a 1ms response time for $299.99 — specs that cost $600+ just two years ago. For the best-performing option near the $400 ceiling, the Acer Nitro XV275U F3 at $379.99 offers 1,152 dimming zones and 320Hz.

**Is 1440p or 1080p better for gaming at 27 inches?**
1440p is clearly better on a 27-inch panel. At 27 inches, 1440p (2560×1440) delivers 109 pixels per inch — text and fine detail in games are noticeably sharper than 1080p's 82 PPI at the same size. Every monitor on this list runs 1440p, and all five pair it with at least 165Hz for smooth motion.

**What is the difference between IPS, VA, and Rapid IPS panels?**
IPS panels offer wide viewing angles (178°) and accurate colors, with a 1,000:1 contrast ratio. VA panels deliver much higher native contrast (2,500:1 to 3,000:1) and deeper blacks, making them better for dark room gaming, but pixel response can be slightly slower in dark-to-dark transitions. Rapid IPS (used in the MSI MAG 274QRFW) is an IPS subtype tuned for faster pixel response, combining IPS color accuracy with improved motion clarity. Mini LED backlights (used in the AOC Q27G3XMN and Acer XV275U F3) boost contrast in any panel type by adding hundreds of individually controllable light zones.

**Does DisplayHDR 400 make a visible difference in games?**
DisplayHDR 400 — found on the LG 27GP850-B, BenQ EX2710Q, and MSI MAG 274QRFW — provides a modest HDR improvement: highlights are brighter than SDR, and tone mapping gives more detail in bright areas. The improvement is visible but not dramatic. For genuinely impactful HDR, you need DisplayHDR 1000 with local dimming zones — which is why the AOC Q27G3XMN (336 zones) and Acer XV275U F3 (1,152 zones) deliver a more striking HDR experience.

**What refresh rate do I need for competitive gaming in 2026?**
For casual and single-player gaming, 165Hz at 1440p is excellent. For competitive FPS titles like Valorant, CS2, or Apex Legends, 180Hz provides a small but real advantage. At the absolute competitive level, the Acer Nitro XV275U F3's 320Hz becomes meaningful — motion is visibly smoother at frame rates above 200 FPS, and pixel response at 0.5ms keeps images sharp through fast movement.
